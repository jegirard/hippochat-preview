Stop Forcing Cross-Org Care Into Single-Org Tools

SMS, pagers, and internal secure-texting systems all fail for the same reason: they weren't designed for communication that crosses organizational boundaries.

The Core Problem

Healthcare communication increasingly happens across organizational lines. External triage nurses, answering services, locum providers, coverage groups—all need to communicate with your clinical team.

But the tools available today were built for a different reality:

  • Secure texting platforms assume everyone is in the same org directory
  • SMS and personal phones have no audit trail or compliance controls
  • Pagers are one-way with no context or accountability
  • "Just give them access" creates security and compliance risks

The result: workarounds, compliance gaps, and communication that falls through the cracks.

Why "Just Give Them Access" Fails

It sounds simple: just add external users to your existing secure messaging system. But this approach creates problems that compound over time:

  • Security risk expansion — Every external user increases your attack surface
  • Offboarding complexity — "Temporary" access becomes permanent
  • Compliance muddiness — Unclear who is responsible for what
  • Directory sprawl — Your internal directory becomes cluttered with external users
