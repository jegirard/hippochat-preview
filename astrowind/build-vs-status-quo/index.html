Build vs Status Quo

Stop Forcing Cross-Org Care Into Single-Org Tools

SMS, pagers, and internal secure-texting systems all fail for the same reason: they weren't designed for the reality of cross-organizational clinical communication.

Request Demo

The Core Problem

Reality check

Healthcare organizations need to communicate with external clinical partners—triage nurses, answering services, coverage physicians, partner clinicians—securely and accountably.

The challenge

But every "solution" creates new problems. None of them were designed for the reality of cross-organizational communication.

Every 'Solution' Creates New Problems

SMS

Fast but non-compliant. PHI on personal devices. No audit trail. A compliance nightmare waiting to happen.

Pagers

Reliable but primitive. No context. No documentation. Callbacks that create more problems than they solve.

Internal Secure Texting

Great for internal use. But giving external users access means IT headaches and blurred organizational boundaries.

EHR Access

"Just give them an Epic account" creates permanent access, credentialing delays, and deprovisioning problems.

Why "Just Give Them Access" Fails

Access doesn't scale

A triage service working with 50 physician clients can't maintain 50 EHR accounts with 50 credential renewal cycles.

Access blurs boundaries

When external partners can see internal patient data, organizational separation breaks down. Minimum necessary access becomes maximum convenient access.

Access creates liability

External users with internal system access muddy the waters when something goes wrong. Who was responsible? What did they see? When?

Access lingers

"Temporary" access has a way of becoming permanent. Deprovisioning is always someone else's problem—until audit.
